Murray N. Rothbard

1926-1995

The founder of modern Libertarianism

Murray Rothbard in the 90s

Here's a timeline of Dr. Rothbard's life:

  • 1926 – Born in Bronx, New York City, New York, United States
  • 1945 – Received a Bachelor of Arts degree in mathematics from Columbia University
  • 1953 – Married JoAnn Schumacher in New York City
  • 1956 – Received a PhD in economics from Columbia University
  • 1962 – Published Man, Economy, and State
  • 1966 – Began teaching economics at Brooklyn Polytechnic Institute
  • 1976 – Founded the Center for Libertarian Studies
  • 1977 – Founded the Journal of Libertarian Studies
  • 1982 – Co-founded the Ludwig von Mises Institute in Auburn, Alabama
  • 1986 – Left BPI to teach at the University of Nevada, Las Vegas where he held the title of S.J. Hall Distinguished Professor of Economics
  • 1987 – Founded the Quarterly Journal of Austrian Economics
  • 1995 – Died (aged 68) in New York City, New York, United States

"And, indeed, what is the State anyway but organized banditry? What is taxation but theft on a gigantic, unchecked, scale? What is war but mass murder on a scale impossible by private police forces? What is conscription but mass enslavement? Can anyone envision a private police force getting away with a tiny fraction of what States get away with, and do habitually, year after year, century after century?"

Murray N. Rothbard, For a New Liberty: The Libertarian Manifesto
